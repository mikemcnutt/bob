Module Overview

What makes a good design for a website? For a business a website is often the first impression s customer has for that business. There are a lot of factors that are involved in design. For this assignment we will consider color theory and design.

Why It Matters

In today's digital age, a business's website often serves as the first point of contact for potential customers. Effective website design builds credibility and trust, making a strong first impression and encouraging users to explore further. Good design is more than just aesthetics; it enhances functionality, user experience, and brand consistency. A well-designed site with a thoughtful layout, clear navigation, and strategic use of color and typography ensures visitors can find information easily and feel confident in the business's professionalism.

Color theory is crucial in web design, as colors evoke emotions, influence perceptions, and drive actions. For instance, blue often conveys trust, while red can create a sense of urgency. Understanding and applying color theory helps create a cohesive and compelling visual experience that aligns with the brand's message. By focusing on these design principles, you'll learn to create websites that not only look appealing but also deliver exceptional user experiences and drive engagement and conversions.
